Here's a simple and authentic Chicken Biryani recipe to get you started:

Ingredients:

For the chicken marinade:

* 1 1/2 pounds boneless, skinless chicken thighs or breast, cut into small pieces
* 1/4 cup plain yogurt
* 1 tablespoon lemon juice
* 1 teaspoon ghee or vegetable oil
* 1 teaspoon cumin powder
* 1 teaspoon coriander powder
* 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
* Salt (optional)

For the biryani:

* 2 cups basmati rice
* 4 cups water
* 2 tablespoons ghee or vegetable oil
* 1 small onion, finely chopped
* 2 cloves garlic, minced
* 1 medium-sized tomato, diced
* 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
* 2 bay leaves
* 3-4 whole cloves
* Salt (optional)
* Green chilies and fresh cilantro for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

1. **Marinate the chicken:** In a large bowl, combine the marinated ingredients (yogurt, lemon juice, ghee or oil, cumin powder, coriander powder, red chili powder) with the chicken pieces. Mix well and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.

2. **Cook the basmati rice:** Rinse the rice in a fine mesh sieve until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in water for about 30 minutes. Drain the water and cook it according to the package instructions or by boiling it with 4 cups of water, covered, using low-medium heat for 15-20 minutes, or until the rice is cooked but still slightly firm.

3. **Make the biryani:** Heat a large, heavy-bottomed vessel (e.g., Dutch oven) over medium-high heat. Add the ghee or oil and swirl it around to coat the bottom. Place the chopped onion and sauté until it turns translucent. Add the minced garlic and sauté for 1 minute.

4. **Add aromatics:** Add the cumin seeds, bay leaves, whole cloves, salt (if using), and diced tomato. Stir well to combine and let them cook for about 5 minutes, or until the onion is softened and the tomato is mushy.

5. **Add chicken and cook:** Add the marinated chicken pieces to the pan and stir gently to coat with the spice mixture. Cook for about 6-8 minutes, or until the chicken is fully cooked and tender.

6. **Assemble the biryani:**
        * In a large microwave-safe bowl, combine half of the cooked basmati rice.
        * Layer the cooked chicken pieces on top of the rice.
        * Spread the remaining half of the cooked basmati rice over the chicken.
7. **Flatten and close:** Press down gently to flatten the biryani layer. Cover it with a tight-fitting lid or foil, ensuring no steam escapes.

8. **Steam the biryani:**
        * Microwave for about 10-12 minutes on high heat.
        * Alternatively, place the vessel in a preheated oven at 350°F (175°C) for 30-40 minutes, or until the aroma of cooked rice and spices fills the air.

9. **Serve:** Remove from the microwave/oven when the biryani has cooked and fluffed up slightly. Serve hot with a dollop of raita (a yogurt-based side dish) or with naan bread for those who enjoy Indian flatbread. Garnish with green chilies, cilantro, or even some crushed onions if you like.

Feel free to adjust the amount of ingredients according to your taste preferences and dietary requirements. Remember to soak the rice properly to avoid a sticky mess!
